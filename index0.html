DO'S

•Wash your hands before putting on the mask and after touching or removing it.

•Wear a mask that fits snuggly against your face.

•Have your mask cover your mouth, nose and chin.

•Wash your mask at least once a day.

•Avoid touching your mask while wearing it.

DON'T

•Don’t wear the mask below your nose or leave your chin uncovered.

•Don’t take off the mask to speak with someone.

•Don’t wear a mask that is damaged.

•Don’t rest your mask under your chin.

•DON’T let the mask provide a false sense of security.You can still get infected by touching your eyes.

DO'S

•Wear gloves while cleaning

•Wash your hands before and after wearing gloves

•Make sure the gloves fit properly before performing the tasks

•Do make sure correct type of gloveis available if you have skin sensitivity

•Wear gloves when taking care of a sick person

DON'T

•Hastily remove your gloves

•Choose the wrong gloves

•Touch your face while wearing gloves

•Don't forget to remove and dispose of gloves properly

•Forget about your personal hygiene.

•Don't use non-approved gloves.

Do's And Don't

•Do use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer. It should contain at least 60% alcohol.They may also contribute to antibiotic resistance.

•Washing with soap and water is better at removing coronavirus.

•If your hands are visibly dirty or greasy, hand sanitizers may not work well. Stick to tried-and-true handwashing.

•It’s unlikely that sanitizers can remove or inactivate chemicals like pesticides and heavy metals.

•If you’ve come in contact with them, wash your hands carefully with soap and water—or as directed by a poison control center.

•Use enough sanitizer to cover all the surfaces of your hands and fingers.Then rub your hands together until they feel dry—that.This should take about 20 seconds

•Do be careful around kids. Keep hand sanitizers out of the reach of young children, who might mistake them for food or candy.

•Don’t rush. Hand sanitizers may not be as effective if you rinse or wipe them off before your hands are dry.
